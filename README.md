/# Schengen zone calculator
## Intro
The Schengen zone calculator is a project I made to make a better calculation of days from the rule of 90/180 inside the Scheguen Zone in Europe. It 
can be really hard for travelers to have a correct idea of how this rule works, and how hard it can become if you are going in and out of the Schengen Zone or 
Schengen Area multiple times. As a traveler, I find it hard to make math and approximate the right and last legal date of departure, which is the reason for this 
application.

## What is the Schengen Zone?

The Schengen Zone, or the Schengen area, now to make it easy, is a formal agreement between the EU countries and other countries in the
European continent guarantees free movement
for mobility, tourism, and business purposes. For more information, you can read this page: https://home-affairs.ec.europa.eu/policies/schengen/schengen-area_en


### The rule 90/180

Now, if you are not a citizen of one of the 29 countries inside the agreement, you are covered under the 90/180 rule. This rule is basically that the
range of time you can stay inside the Schengen Zone is 90 days within a range of 180 days. This means you have to count 180 days before the date x and 
add the number of days you have been inside the Zone, and it must be less than 90 days. Of course, if you get inside once into the Zone and stay 90 days,
it's not so hard to make the count by hand. The problem starts when you enter multiple times; this can make the calculation a problem, and the legal time 
a guessing game, and not a certain, countable, and logical thing.

## Description
The Schengen Zone calculator helps to make these calculations easier to handle using date ranges, initial, and final dates to make it understandable. This tool
can make a one-time entrance, multiple entrances, or date range calculations, giving you the exact days of stay, the max date of legal stay, and the possible
date you can enter with the full 90 days deppending on the last date joined.
